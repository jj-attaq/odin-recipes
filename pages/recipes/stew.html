Super Simple Savory Stew


You will need the following ingredients:

  • 500 - 750g of grass fed beef in small chunks, preferably with some fat
  • 5 to 7 organic red bell peppers
  • 2 or 3 big organic carrots
  • 2 or 3 organic parsley roots
  • 1kg of organic onion
  • 500g or so of organic potato or sweet potato
  • 5 or 6 organic green onions
  • a bottle of red table wine
  • half a head of garlic
  • spices like red pepper powder and even cayenne if you want a spicier stew
  • rosemary
  • salt and pepper
  • butter

Once you have all of your ingredients washed and prepared, we can get started!

  1. start by chopping up all the produce and putting them in separate bowls
  2. grab your biggest pot and set it on the stove to a medium high temperature
  3. put in some butter and while it's melting get your beef ready
  4. the following could take a while, so feel free to dip into the wine a little and put on some music
    • put in only a few beef chunks into the sizzling butter at a time
    • we're trying to 'brown' all the meat, leaving a bit of brown in the pot,
      but not cooking the meat through, that comes later
    • it shouldn't take more than a minute to brown each small batch of beef
    • once that's done, we can move on to the next step!
  5. add in a bit more butter to the pot and once melted,
    dump in the onions, garlic, rosemary, salt and pepper
  6. stir the onions while they sizzle, once they're starting to get transluscent, it's time to move on
  7. put in your peppers and green onions now too and stir for a few minutes
  8. put in the carrots and parsley roots
  9. add in about half the bottle of wine and stir it all together for a few minutes
  10. bring the temperature down and dump in the potato/sweet potatoes
    and add the meat back into the mix
  11. put in the rest of the wine and add only enough water to cover everything
  12. your stew should never boil, only simmer
  13. now we play the waiting game
  14. come back to your stew every 20 - 30 minutes and give it a good stir
  15. once the liquid has started evaporating and become more 'saucy'
    you're done!
  16. serve and enjoy!
